Frequently Asked Questions

Marathi is a language spoken mainly in the Indian state of Maharashtra, and is the fourth most spoken language in India. Marathi text extractors typically use optical character recognition (OCR) technology to recognize Marathi characters and convert them into machine-readable text.

OCR stands for Optical Character Recognition. It is a technology that allows computers to recognize and extract text from images or scanned documents.

Marathi OCR is an OCR system that can recognize and extract Marathi text from images or scanned documents.

Marathi OCR can be used in a variety of applications, such as document scanning, automatic translation, and text analysis.

The accuracy of Marathi OCR can be affected by factors such as the quality of the input source, the complexity of the font used, and the presence of noise or distortion in the input image.

Tesseract OCR is a popular open-source OCR library that supports Marathi language.
